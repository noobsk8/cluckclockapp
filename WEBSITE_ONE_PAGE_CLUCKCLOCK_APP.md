## Selling points (above the fold or first scroll)

1. **Alarms that need you** — Your chicken crows at the time you set. To turn it off you feed it: tap the three worm plots in a 2×2 grid. Skip or snooze too much and the chicken loses health. One chicken for weekdays, one for weekends.
2. **Real consequences** — Healthy → Thin → Malnourished → Dead. Each chicken has three lives. Fail (skip, or feed 0–1 worms) and it moves one step closer to death. Succeed (3 worms) and it recovers. Death is permanent; you start a new chicken with a new personality and name.
3. **Built to actually ring** — Notifications at alarm time, optional in-app crowing through the speaker (even on silent), background audio so the sound keeps playing if you switch apps. Snooze = 5 minutes, then it crows again.
4. **More than alarms** — Weekday Guardian and Weekend Rooster for Mon–Fri and Sat–Sun. Plus a stopwatch (Sprinter) and a countdown timer that notifies you when it hits zero, even if the app is in the background.
5. **Your coop, your style** — Name your chickens, meet their random personalities (the Early Bird, the Grumpy, etc.), and pick how the app looks: System, Light, Dark, Game Boy Grey, Game Boy Red, or image backgrounds (Chip, Clock) with a glass-style card over the coop.

## App flows (for “How it works” or feature section)

### Daily alarm flow

1. **Set alarms** — Choose Weekday Guardian (Mon–Fri) or Weekend Rooster (Sat–Sun), add one or more alarms, set time and repeat days.
2. **Alarm fires** — Notification at the set time. Tap it to open the app (or hear in-app crowing if the app is in the background).
3. **Full-screen overlay** — Your chicken is crowing. You can:
   - **Feed** — Open the worm game, tap the 3 plots that have worms (2×2 grid). Score 3 = chicken gains health; 2 = no change; 0–1 = chicken loses health.
   - **Snooze** — Swipe down; chicken “head in ground” for 5 minutes, then it crows again. Snoozing caps how many worms count (e.g. one snooze = max 2 plots that count).
   - **Skip** — Dismiss without playing; counts as a failure and the chicken loses health.
4. **Resolution** — After you feed or skip, that chicken is “resolved” for the day. The overlay dismisses. Next alarm for that chicken can fire again the next day.

### Health and stakes

- **Per chicken** — Weekday and Weekend each have their own health. Only the chicken whose alarm fired is affected when you feed or skip.
- **States** — Healthy → Thin → Malnourished → Dead. Three failures (e.g. skip or bad feed) in a row and the chicken dies.
- **Recovery** — Feeding 3 worms moves the chicken one step toward Healthy. Snoozing 3+ times in one alarm session adds an extra health penalty.
- **Death** — Permanent. You start a new chicken with a new random personality and suggested name.

### Stopwatch and timer

- **Sprinter** — In-app stopwatch: start, stop, lap, reset. Time doesn’t run in the background; on return the display syncs from when you left.
- **Timer** — Set a countdown; start it. When it hits zero you get a “Timer done” notification even if the app is in the background.
