CluckClock vs the default iPhone alarm

If the built-in iPhone alarm is too easy to snooze or ignore, CluckClock adds a more active wake-up loop with mini-games, virtual pet accountability, and weekday/weekend separation.

Download CluckClock

Why people switch

The default iPhone alarm is simple and reliable, but it is also easy to dismiss half-awake. CluckClock is built for people who want more friction between being asleep and turning an alarm off.

What CluckClock adds

  • Mini-games to end the alarm
  • Separate weekday and weekend alarm modes
  • Virtual pet accountability tied to chicken health
  • Permanent Graveyard history
  • Shareable live and dead chicken screens

Choose CluckClock if...

  • You regularly snooze or dismiss the built-in iPhone alarm without fully waking up.
  • You want the alarm to make you do something before it ends, instead of giving you a fast one-tap escape.
  • You like the idea of a stronger routine hook with mini-games, accountability to a virtual pet, and shareable progress.
  • You want separate weekday and weekend alarm identities rather than one simple alarm list.

Stick with the default iPhone alarm if...

  • You want the simplest possible alarm with no extra game, pet, or consequence layer.
  • You already wake up reliably and do not need extra friction to stop snoozing.
  • You prefer a minimal built-in tool over a more playful app with stronger personality.
